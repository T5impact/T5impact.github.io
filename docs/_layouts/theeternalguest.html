Overview

The Eternal Guest is a narrative-driven exploration game set within a mysterious hotel where rooms shift and reconfigure over time. The project focused heavily on modular player systems, systemic progression, and tightly integrated combat, inventory, and ability frameworks that could support a wide variety of playstyles.

I served as the primary gameplay programmer and team lead, owning the majority of player-facing systems, enemy and boss AI behavior, and multiple core refactors driven by playtesting feedback. I also contributed significantly to the game’s visual presentation through custom shaders, VFX, and UI design.

Technical Challenges & Solutions

Refactoring Exploration Without Breaking Design Intent

Playtesting revealed that the original fully random layout caused excessive disorientation. I led a refactor that introduced consistent structural generation with a probabilistic chance for doors to desynchronize, paired with periodic layout resets. This maintained the non-Euclidean theme while improving player comprehension and flow.

Designing Scalable Ability Architecture

To support a wide variety of abilities with unique behaviors and visuals, I designed a prefab-based polymorphic system where each ability encapsulates its logic and presentation. This allowed new abilities to be added with minimal engineering overhead and no changes to core player logic.
