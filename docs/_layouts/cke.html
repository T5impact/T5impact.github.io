CKE

Technical Artist / Gameplay Programmer — 48-Hour Game Jam

  • Duration: 48-hour Game Jam
  • Team: 4 developers
  • Engine: Unity (C#, HLSL)
  • Platforms: PC (Itch)
  • Theme: Contradiction

Overview

CKE is a top-down gliding puzzle game inspired by the clean, clinical aesthetic of Portal. The player simultaneously controls two mirrored versions of the same character across separate maps, with one character’s controls inverted.

To complete a level, the player must guide both characters to their exits while collecting all cakes on one map and actively avoiding cakes on the other—an intentional mechanical contradiction.

Core Mechanics

  • Dual-character control with inverted input mapping
  • Top-down gliding movement with momentum-based steering
  • Asymmetric objectives across synchronized levels
  • Precision-focused puzzle design built around contradiction

Art Direction & Technical Art

I was responsible for all visual development, including art direction, asset creation, and shader implementation. The goal was to achieve a Portal-inspired visual language while remaining readable in a top-down puzzle context.

  • Designed and implemented multiple custom shaders under 48-hour constraints
  • Created stylized surface shaders for floors, walls, and interactables
  • Built emissive and outline effects to guide player attention
  • Balanced visual clarity against aesthetic cohesion during rapid iteration

Level Progression

I designed the level progression to introduce mechanics incrementally, allowing players to internalize the contradiction-based ruleset without explicit tutorialization.

  • Early levels focus on mirrored movement comprehension
  • Mid-game levels introduce cake collection vs. avoidance tension
  • Later levels combine tight navigation with visual misdirection

Key Takeaways

  • Strong visual direction can be achieved through shader iteration alone
  • Technical art decisions must prioritize readability under time pressure
  • Mechanics and narrative are most effective when inseparable
