Overview

Pac’s Revenge is a retro-inspired first-person shooter developed over two years and released on Steam. I served as the team lead and primary gameplay programmer, owning core combat systems, enemy AI, progression, and custom rendering features.

My Responsibilities

  • Designed and implemented modular combat and weapon systems
  • Built enemy AI using state-based and rule-driven behaviors
  • Developed player progression, inventory, and stat systems
  • Created custom post-processing and full-screen shaders (HLSL)
  • Led technical decision-making and code architecture

Key Gameplay Systems

Combat & Weapon Architecture

Weapons were implemented using an abstract base class and data-driven configuration, allowing new weapons to be added without modifying existing combat logic.

Enemy AI

Enemy behaviors were structured as modular state machines with shared perception and decision logic, enabling reuse across enemy types while supporting unique behavior patterns.

Progression & Player Stats

Player progression and stat scaling were implemented as isolated systems to avoid hard dependencies on combat logic, reducing coupling and easing iteration during balance passes.

Technical Challenges & Solutions

  • Refactored allocation-heavy gameplay loops to eliminate GC spikes during combat encounters.
  • Optimized AI update frequency and perception checks to maintain stable frame rates with multiple active enemies.
  • Built custom post-processing shaders to achieve a retro aesthetic while minimizing render cost.
