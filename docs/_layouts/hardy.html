H.A.R.D.Y

Gameplay Programmer — Game Jam Project

  • Duration: 1-Month Game Jam
  • Team: 1 developer
  • Engine: Unity (C#)
  • Platforms: PC (Itch)
  • Theme: Time

Overview

H.A.R.D.Y is a physics-driven action game created during a 1-month game jam, and it was my first game ever created. The player must deliver packages across various environments while avoiding pirates and police and facing a strict time constraint. The project emphasized emergent gameplay, readable physics interactions, and designing mechanics that remained intuitive under rapid iteration.

My Contributions

  • Implemented core player movement and physics interactions
  • Developed ability systems tied to environmental manipulation
  • Handled input, camera behavior, and moment-to-moment feel
  • Worked closely with design to tune mechanics in real time

Design Constraints & Solutions

  • Balanced simulation accuracy with responsiveness to maintain player control
  • Simplified physics interactions to reduce unpredictable edge cases
  • Scoped mechanics to ensure stability and polish within the jam timeframe

Gameplay Footage

Key Takeaways

  • Physics-based mechanics require aggressive constraint to feel reliable
  • Rapid tuning is more valuable than complex systems under time pressure
  • Player readability should always trump simulation fidelity in jam contexts
